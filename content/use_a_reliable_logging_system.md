[[you_need_to_curate_your_garden|You need to curate your garden]], or entropy **will** win. The typical set was getting cluttered because there was no buffer between ideas that pop in my head and the website (I [[publish_your_notes_instantly|publish my notes instantly]]). Yet, I still felt that I was losing a lot of material: I could not type notes when I was on the go. I needed a log to dump everything.

<figure>
<img src="img/use-reliable-logging.jpeg" />

</figure>

The log serves two puposes:

- A lot of low-quality material, half-baked ideas don't make it here;
- On the other hand, I *know* everything is in there and that I can retrieve it later.

I originally tried solutions that were closer to the technology I use for this site ([logseq](https://logseq.com) allows to edit and export org files). But I am optimizing for **efficiency** here (and you should too), not for technological compatibility. I just want to log the thought or the reference in a few seconds, and move on with my life. Integration with this website can be done when I am on my computer.

I use Notion which. It has a mobile app, and does export the log as a webpage: [here is the log](https://soundslab.notion.site/soundslab/Log-c873cc5909e54faf91590c68a587841c).
