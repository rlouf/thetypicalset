Democracy (the vote) mediates the opinion of everyone to make a decision over one thing. Where a decentralized system would have led to more chaos. There is a tradeoff to be found between entropy reduction (ultimately authoritarian) and completely chaotic. I guess that's what designing representative systems and voting systems is about. [[complexity_is_an_issue_for_societies|Complexity is an issue for societies]]

Decentralizing decision too much leads to an unnecessary increase in entropy. In this sense "the market" (whatever this means, [[there_is_no_such_thing_as_the_market|there is no such thing as "the market"]]) cannot be an efficient solution. Asking everyone at a given point in time what they want and enforcing that choice is the best information transfer mechanism we can think of. This seems to be an argument in favor of *more democracy*, as in Switzerland.
