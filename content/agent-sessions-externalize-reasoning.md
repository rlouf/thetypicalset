---
title: "Agent sessions make reasoning explicit"
---

When a coding agent works on a problem, it produces a sequential record of its
reasoning process as a structural byproduct. Not as an optional feature — as an
intrinsic property of how agents operate. The exploration, the dead ends, the
decisions, the uncertainty are all recorded in the [[session-traces-artifact|session trace]]. This is
qualitatively new. Human developers reason as carefully as agents do, but that
reasoning is internal and largely unrecoverable. Agent reasoning is external and
fully recoverable. For the first time, the gap between "thinking happened" and
"thinking is available" closes automatically, without requiring the author to do
anything extra.
