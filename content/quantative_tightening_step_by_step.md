This is a follow-up on [[quantitative_easing_step_by_step|Quantitative Easing step-by-step]].

The chain goes as follows:

1.  Investor wants to buy treasuries with deposit (swap)
2.  Bank settles payment (deposit -> reserve) with treasuries \| reduces reserves and deposit liabilities
3.  Treasury issues new UST -> gets payment in reserve -> pays Fed for UST \| nothing changes
4.  Fed extinguishes the reserves

- The Fed has no idea what the QT is actually doing, and has [[monetary_policy_report_july_2019|changed strategy several times before stopping]]
- The Fed had no idea how much reserve banks need
- [[banks_have_too_much_reserve|Banks have too much reserve]]
- Fed stopped QT because [Repo rate exploded](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/SOFR) (it has no idea why) and even restarted QE
