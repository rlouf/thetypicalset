Summer vacations is coming to an end and though I have left school for more than 10 years, I have kept a habit of setting my technical and learning goals in September. I set personal and professional goals in January.

# The past year in review

Let's review the past year briefly. It has been fruitful:

- The projects I am a part of (the [Aesara ecosystem](https://github.com/aesara-devs), [Blackjax](https://github.com/blackjax-devs/blackjax), [Pollsposition](https://github.com/pollsposition)) have made sensible progress and are gaining momentum.
- Working in close proximity with great people, including but not only (in handle alphabetical order) [@BrandonTWillard](https://twitter.com/BrandonTWillard), [@dgerlanc](https://twitter.com/dgerlanc), [@jeffreydenos](https://twitter.com/jeffreydenos) and [@junpenglao](https://twitter.com/junpenglao), has made me a better developer.
- Thanks to [@BrandonTWillard](https://twitter.com/BrandonTWillard) I have now fully transitioned from vim to emacs.
- I have picked up [Racket](https://racket-lang.org/);
- I learned about relational programming, in particular [[minikanren|miniKanren]].
- My thoughts on efficient note-taking, knowledge sharing and what a "better Internet" would look like are getting clearer, thanks to the [typical set project](https://thetypicalset.com), which I set in motion this year.
- I predicted the results of the French presidential elections with [@alex~andorra~](https://twitter.com/alex_andorra) and [@pollsposition](https://twitter.com/pollsposition) (although a serious injury in February prevented me from doing more). I went on TV the night of the election!

# Hello 2022/2023

Here are the goals that I would try to achieve this coming year:

- [AeMCMC](https://github.com/aesara-devs/aemcmc) can build samplers with 99% of models encountered in the wild;
- [AeHMC](https://github.com/aesara-devs/aehmc)'s NUTS sampler's performance is on par with Blackjax's;
- [Aesara](https://github.com/aesara-devs/aesara) uses [[e_graphs|e-graphs]] and equality saturation for its graph rewrites;
- [Blackjax](https://github.com/blackjax-devs/blackjax)'s V1 is released;
- The Blackjax paper is ready for submission;
- [miniKanren's python implementation](https://github.com/pythological/kanren) [[solve-sudokus-kanren|solves sudoku grid]] almost as fast as the imperative solution;
- Implementation of something similar to [WaveFunctionCollapse](https://github.com/mxgmn/WaveFunctionCollapse) with miniKanren;
- The typical set is self-hosted on the most energy efficient stack possible.

And more generally improve thetypicalset's design, navigation and overall content quality. I would like to learn some [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/) and learn more about internet protocols (why not implement a HTTP server in Rust?) if time allows.
